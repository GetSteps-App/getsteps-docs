## Workout Stats

Every workout detail view shows:

- **Duration** - Total workout time
- **Distance** - Total distance covered
- **Pace** - Average pace (for walking and running)
- **Elevation Gain** - Total climb during the workout
- **Heart Rate** - Average and max heart rate
- **Calories** - Active energy burned
- **Weather** - Temperature and humidity during the workout

## Heart Rate & Zones

When a workout has heart rate data, the **Heart Rate** section shows your average and max along with a chart of your heart rate over the session.

Below the chart, the **Time in Zones** breakdown shows how long you spent in each of five heart rate zones, from easy effort to maximum effort:

| Zone | Effort |
|------|--------|
| **Zone 1** | Very light |
| **Zone 2** | Light |
| **Zone 3** | Moderate |
| **Zone 4** | Hard |
| **Zone 5** | Maximum |

Each zone shows the time spent in it and its heart rate range in bpm.

For distance-based workouts, the **Splits** table breaks your workout down by distance — one row per kilometer or mile, depending on your unit setting. Each split shows:

- **Pace** (or **Speed** for cycling)
- **Time** — Cumulative elapsed time at the end of the split
- **HR** — Average heart rate for the split, when available

A bar next to each split makes it easy to compare your fastest and slowest segments at a glance.

## Route Playback

<Note>Route playback is a Pro feature.</Note>

{/* TODO: Screenshot of 3D route playback with the follow camera */}
<Frame caption="3D route playback following the route with a cinematic camera">
  <img src="/images/screenshots/activity-detail-route-playback.jpg" alt="3D route playback" />
</Frame>

Replay your workout route in 3D to relive how you moved through your session. A cinematic follow camera swoops in and then glides along your path, smoothing through every turn as it tracks your position.

You can tailor the playback:

- **Map style** — switch between **Standard** and **Satellite**. Both render realistic elevation during playback for a true 3D view.
- **Speed** — adjust the playback duration from **15 to 180 seconds**. Steps picks a sensible default based on the distance of your workout, and you can speed it up or slow it down to taste.

## GPX Export

<Note>GPX export is a Pro feature.</Note>

Export your workout route as a `.gpx` file to use in other fitness apps or mapping tools. Tap the share button and select GPX export to save or send the file.

## Deleting a Workout

To remove a workout, tap the menu button (⋯) in the top-right corner and choose **Delete**. You'll be asked to confirm, since this removes the workout from both Steps and Apple Health.

<Warning>Deleting a workout removes it from Apple Health as well, and it can't be undone.</Warning>
